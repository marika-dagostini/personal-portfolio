About me

I'm a PhD Student in Statistical Sciences at the University of Bologna, where I also work as a teaching tutor in the Department of Statistical Sciences "Paolo Fortunati".

My research interests lie in Bayesian hierarchical models, particularly spatial and spatio-temporal modelling, with applications in environmental and epidemiological studies. I am also interested in data visualization and enjoy creating web map applications to present spatial data in a clear and interactive way.

What i'm researching

  • Urban Heat Islands

    Bayesian hierarchical modeling to quantify urban heat islands and develop high-temperature exposure maps for epidemiological research.

  • Vulnerability Indicators

    Bayesian latent factor models to construct composite indicators of environmental and climate vulnerability.

  • Disease Mapping

    Extending disease mapping models to address the unique spatial challenges posed by small and irregularly shaped areas.

  • Downscaling in Remote Sensing

    Bayesian hierarchical models to downscale low-resolution remote sensing data to improve exposure assessment in epidemiological studies.

  • Data Visualization

    Developing web-based mapping applications to communicate research findings in a clear and accessible way.

  • Knitting

    Perhaps not (yet) a formal academic discipline, but one with more maths and trial-and-error research than one might expect.
